Year in Review

The 2015-16 school year was filled with incredible highs for the MSU community, such as MSU football and men's basketball winning the Big Ten championship and MSU football's miracle win over the University of Michigan in Ann Arbor, but the school year was not without its troubles. From issues of sexual assault policy issues at MSU to a young man who tragically took his own life, the MSU community had its emotions tested.

Investigation finds MSU mishandled sexual assault cases

The United States Department of Education's Office of Civil Rights came to the conclusion that MSU did not resolve two separate sexual assault cases quickly enough in accordance to OCR mandates — the findings came after a year-and-a-half investigation from the Office of Civil Rights. After the announcement, MSU released a list of steps it planned to take to improve the timeliness of sexual assault case investigations.

Federal report reveals MSU counselor accused of sexual harassment allowed to keep job four years after

One of the findings from the Office of Civil Rights’ investigation into MSU sexual assault policy was of an MSU counselor who was accused of sexually harassing students who were seeking counseling after being sexually assaulted. After no action was allegedly taken against the counselor after the first incident, MSU launched a Title IX investigation into the accusations after more people filed reports against the counselor in 2013. When the investigation began the counselor was removed from contact with students and relieved of clinical responsibilities while the investigation was ongoing.
